The correct answer is: 'Player B may earn any of the following amounts of points: 0, 6 or 24.'.


Explanation

Group 1
Player Player's Multiplier Player's Bid
A 2 10
B 3 6
C 6 8
Group 2
Player Player's Multiplier Player's Bid
D 2 1
E 4 1
F 10 1

Player B bid 6 and the other players in his group bid 10 and 8. Thus, player B is the lowest bidder in his group.

In Group 2, all the three players bid the same value. Therefore, the computer will randomly determine the ranking of their bids.

If the computer determines D is the lowest bidder in his group, then player B will be paired with player D and his score will be:

(B's multiplier)×(D's multiplier)−(B's bid) = 3×2−6 = 6-6=0

If the computer determines E is the lowest bidder in his group, then player B will be paired with player E and his score will be:

(B's multiplier)×(E's multiplier)−(B's bid) = 3×4−6 = 12-6=6

If the computer determines F is the lowest bidder in his group, then player B will be paired with player F and his score will be:

(B's multiplier)×(F's multiplier)−(B's bid) = 3×10−6 = 30-6=24

Thus, player B may earn any of these three scores, depending on the random choice of the computer.
