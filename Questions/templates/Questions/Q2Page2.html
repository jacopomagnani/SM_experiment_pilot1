The correct answer is: 'Player B may earn either 10 points or 14 points.'.


Explanation

Group 1
Player Player's Multiplier Player's Bid
A 9 0
B 2 0
C 6 15
Group 2
Player Player's Multiplier Player's Bid
D 5 2
E 2 12
F 7 7

Player B bid 0 and the other players in his group bid 0 and 15. Player C is the highest bidder. Players A and B bid the same value and therefore the computer will randomly determine who is the second-highest bidder and who is the lowest bidder.

In Group 2, the highest bidder is player E, the second-highest bidder is player F and the lowest bidder is player D.

If the computer determines B is the second-highest bidder in his group, then he will be paired with player F and his score will be:

(B's multiplier)×(F's multiplier)−(B's bid) = 2×7−0 = 14-0=14

If the computer determines B is the lowest bidder in his group, then he will be paired with player D and his score will be:

(B's multiplier)×(D's multiplier)−(B's bid) = 2×5−0 = 10-0=10

Thus, player B may earn either of these two scores, depending on the random choice of the computer. However, player B has no chance to earn 4 points. That would happen only if he was ranked at the top and paired with player E.
