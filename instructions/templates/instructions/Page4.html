ROUNDS, MARKETS AND GROUPS
{% endblock %} {% block content %}

  • Part 1 of the experiment will be divided into 40 rounds.
  • Decisions and points you make in one round do not affect other rounds.
  • All the participants in the room will be divided into groups of six individuals. Each group of six participants is called a market.
  • During the experiment you will only interact with the other five members of your market.
  • In each round, the six members of a market will be randomly divided into two groups with 3 participants each, called Group 1 and Group 2.
  • Thus, in each round you will be randomly assigned to either Group 1 or Group 2 in your market.
